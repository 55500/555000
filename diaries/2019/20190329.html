A friend recently turned me to the idea of avoiding using the internet as a means of entertainment. I immediately attached myself to the idea. There are a number of reasons why I find this appealing.

Least importantly, it has the power to make the media I do consume much more impactful to my memory. This is in part due to traditional/physical media (ie. not on-demand) requiring more effort on the consumer to initiate the act of consumption, as well as an overall more limited library of options. Personally, having the media I consume be more impactful and longer-lasting in my memory is very appealing.

A more important result of eliminating internet-borne entertainment is the enhancement of the internet as a utility. Considering the internet has been shaped into a wasteland of net-culture (memes) and advertising, having the internet become a useful tool at my disposal rather than another distraction in my life is a welcome change. By changing how I use the internet, far-reaching services such as Google (Search, Ad-Sense, etc.) should notice my usage change and begin to change how it is presented to me, tailoring it more to my utilitarian needs rather than entertainment.

At this point, the ultimate outcome for me is a reduced reliance on the internet as a need to get by in my day. Downtime is often Internet Time for me, which amounts to scrolling through news headlines I don't care about or revisiting homepages I've already visited multiple times that day (or that minute) just to pass the time. Usually my Internet Time is more of a "fidget" activity than it is a mentally stimulating one. I see this as a very negative way to spend my time. Outside of the moment, I would much rather close my eyes and think about, well, anything than fidget my way through the internet.
